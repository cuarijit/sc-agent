# 5. Critical Operational Challenges Identified

## 5.1 Route-to-Market Execution ("Salesman Planning")

The team highlighted a clear gap between planning and execution:

> "We have a plan, but it is not translating to what actually sells in the market."
>
> "Sales, supply, and planning are not fully connected."

At route level, trucks are currently loaded based on experience rather than precise demand visibility.

**In practice:**

- Product mix is not always aligned to actual demand by route
- Some stock returns unsold (approx 10%), while other opportunities are missed
- Sell-through varies depending on individual judgement

### As Expressed by the Team

> "We need something that tells us what we should do."
>
> "We want to understand what each route should carry."

### What This Means

The issue is not distribution capacity — it is **loadability and execution precision**.

The key requirement is the ability to determine:

- What should go on each truck
- Which SKUs should be prioritised by route
- How to maximise sell-through on every trip

### Underlying Gap

High-level plans are not yet translated into SKU-level, route-level loading decisions.

---

## 5.2 Milk Utilisation & Product Allocation

As discussed:

> "We need to know how much milk we are going to receive… and what we should do with it."

### Current Reality

- Milk supply is known
- Decisions on utilisation are not fully optimised
- Excess is managed operationally
- Product mix decisions are often reactive

### As Expressed by the Team

> "We are managing the situation, but we need a better way to decide."
>
> "We need to decide earlier what to do, not after."

### What This Means

The issue is not supply — it is **value optimisation**.
